Tax & VAT Guides

What is VAT in South Africa?

Value-Added Tax (VAT) in South Africa is a 15% tax on most goods and services, collected by businesses for SARS. Introduced in 1991, it funds public services like roads and schools. If an item costs R100, you pay R115 R15 goes to SARS. Businesses with over R1 million turnover must register, file returns, and can claim input tax credits. How SARS Tax Works

SARS taxes income progressively higher earners pay more. For 2025, rates start at 18% (up to R237,100) and hit 45% (over R1,817,000). PAYE deducts tax monthly, UIF takes 1% (capped at R177.12), and medical aid credits (R364 main member, R246 per extra dependent) reduce your taxable income.
